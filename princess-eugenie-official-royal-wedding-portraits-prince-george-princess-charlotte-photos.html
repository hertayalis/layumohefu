    Princess Eugenie's Official Wedding Portraits Feature Prince George &amp; Princess Charlotte

    After a busy day at the royal wedding, Prince George and Princess Charlotte were probably ready for an afternoon nap. Instead, they needed to be on their best behavior, as both were required to pose in Princess Eugenie's official wedding portraits.

    16 Aug 2024, 00:00

    After a busy day at the royal wedding, Prince George and Princess Charlotte were probably ready for an afternoon nap. Instead, they needed to be on their best behavior, as both were required to pose in Princess Eugenie's official wedding portraits.

    Related Story

    Unsurprisingly, Princess Eugenie and Jack Brooksbank's wedding photos are completely gorgeous. The incredible wedding snaps were captured by renowned photographer Alex Bramall, who previously photographed Princess Eugenie for Harper's BAZAAR back in 2016. And for her evening party, Eugenie changed into a flawless Zac Posen gown that completely stole the show.

    With six young bridesmaids and two page boys as part of their wedding party, Eugenie and Jack were likely prepared for an array of moods when it came to trying to get their all important wedding photos. And despite being so well behaved throughout Eugenie's wedding ceremony, Kate Middleton and Prince William's children, Prince George and Princess Charlotte, were definitely flagging a little when it came to posing later in the day.

    Alex Bramall/MEGA

    In the same shot as their great-grandparents, Queen Elizabeth and Prince Philip, Charlotte and George weren't their usual smiley selves in the main family photo:

    Alex Bramall/MEGA

    Bless them!

    But when it came to a photo that only included the bride, groom, and the young bridal party, Princess Charlotte and Prince George both seemed to cheer up.

    Alex Bramall/MEGA

    In fact, George began positively beaming, proving that he's a royal professional through and through. And even though Princess Charlotte's smile was much more subdued than usual, she was definitely happy to be included in 2018's second royal wedding, alongside her brother:

    Alex Bramall/MEGA

    As always, the royal kids stole the show, just as they do at practically every other event they go to.
